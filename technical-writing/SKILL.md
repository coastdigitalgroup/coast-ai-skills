# Technical Writing

Write so the reader can act with confidence.

## Core Approach

- Start from the reader's task, not the author's internal model.
- Prefer concrete steps and examples over abstract description.
- Keep terminology consistent throughout the document.
- Surface prerequisites, assumptions, and failure cases early.
- Cut filler that does not help the reader decide or act.

## Working Pattern

1. Identify the reader and what they need to accomplish.
2. Lead with the most useful context or task path.
3. Break long explanations into short, navigable sections.
4. Add examples where ambiguity would slow the reader down.
5. Revise for clarity, accuracy, and scannability.

## Guardrails

- Avoid vague verbs such as "handle" or "manage" when a specific action is known.
- Avoid mixing conceptual explanation and step-by-step procedure without signaling the shift.
- Avoid assuming hidden knowledge.
- Prefer precise language over marketing language.

## Output Expectations

- Produce documentation that is easy to scan.
- Keep examples realistic and minimal.
- Make next actions obvious.
