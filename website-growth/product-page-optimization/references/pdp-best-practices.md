# PDP Optimization Best Practices & Heuristics

## The "Golden Triangle" of Conversion

The most important area of a Product Detail Page is the triangle between the
**Product Image**, the **Price**, and the **Add-to-Cart Button**.

- Users scan the image for "desirability."
- They scan the price for "feasibility."
- They scan the button for "actionability." If these three elements are not
  visually connected, cognitive load increases.

## UX Heuristics for Product Pages

### 1. The F-Pattern (Desktop)

Users typically scan in an F-pattern:

1. Horizontal scan across the top (Product Name/Reviews).
2. Vertical scan down the left (Main Images).
3. Horizontal scan across the middle (Price/ATC).

### 2. Thumb Zone (Mobile)

On mobile, the "Add to Cart" button should ideally be a "Sticky CTA" that stays
at the bottom of the screen as the user scrolls. This ensures that no matter how
much they read, the action is always 1 tap away.

### 3. Progressive Disclosure

Don't overwhelm the user with a giant wall of text.

- **Top:** Summary benefit and price.
- **Middle:** Scannable bullet points.
- **Bottom:** Deep technical specs, detailed reviews, and shipping FAQs.

## Persuasion Principles for PDPs

- **Social Proof (Wisdom of the Crowd):** "14 people are looking at this item
  right now" or "500 sold this month."
- **Scarcity (Loss Aversion):** "Limited Edition" or "Sale ends in 4 hours."
- **Authority:** "As seen in [Major Publication]" or "Approved by [Industry
  Expert]."
- **Unity:** "Join 50,000 other [Persona Name]s who love this product."

## Visual Hierarchy Rules

- **The ATC Button should be the "Brightest" thing on the page.** If your brand
  color is blue, use orange or green for the button to make it "pop."
- **Variant selection should be "Chunked."** Group sizes together, group colors
  together. Avoid making the user hunt for the right selector.
