# Social Proof Audit Checklist

Use this checklist to evaluate the effectiveness of trust signals on any
high-value webpage.

## 1. Inventory & Health Check

- [ ] **Presence:** Is there at least one form of social proof above the fold?
- [ ] **Recency:** Are testimonials or case studies from the last 12-18 months?
- [ ] **Realism:** Do testimonials have real names, titles, and photos (not
      stock)?
- [ ] **Linkability:** If using third-party badges (G2, Trustpilot), do they
      link to the actual review profile?

## 2. Content Quality (The "Specificity" Test)

- [ ] **The "So What?" Test:** Does every quote mention a specific outcome or
      solved pain point?
- [ ] **Bolding:** Are the most impactful sentences bolded for scannability?
- [ ] **Diversity:** Do the proof points represent different segments of your
      target audience?
- [ ] **Objection Handling:** Do any testimonials specifically address common
      fears (e.g., "I thought the setup would be hard, but...")?

## 3. Strategic Placement

- [ ] **CTA Proximity:** Is there a trust signal (e.g., "5-star rating," "No
      credit card required," "Trusted by X") within 50px of the main button?
- [ ] **Friction Points:** Are there security badges or privacy guarantees on
      forms or checkout pages?
- [ ] **Contextual Relevance:** Does the proof on the "Features" page relate to
      those specific features?

## 4. Visual Execution

- [ ] **Hierarchy:** Is the social proof supporting the main message, not
      distracting from it?
- [ ] **Mobile Optimization:** Are testimonial sliders or logo grids easy to
      swipe and read on small screens?
- [ ] **Consistency:** Do the styles of the logos and review blocks match the
      site's design system?

---

## Audit Result Scoring

| Score     | Status   | Action                                                                                   |
| --------- | -------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **0-5**   | Critical | Site lacks credibility. Focus on gathering 3 high-quality case studies immediately.      |
| **6-10**  | Weak     | Proof is present but vague. Refine copy for specificity and improve placement near CTAs. |
| **11-14** | Strong   | Good use of proof. Focus on adding video testimonials or high-authority industry badges. |
| **15+**   | Elite    | Excellence in trust building. Use A/B testing to find the perfect mix of proof points.   |
