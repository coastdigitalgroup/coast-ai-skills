# Conversion Flow Patterns & Frameworks

This document outlines standard frameworks for organizing landing page content
to maximize persuasion and logical flow.

## 1. Problem-Agitation-Solution (PAS)

Best for: Users who are **Problem Aware** but may not realize the severity or
that a solution exists.

- **Problem:** Define the pain point the user is currently experiencing.
- **Agitation:** Deepen the pain. Explain the consequences of _not_ solving the
  problem (wasted time, lost money, stress).
- **Solution:** Introduce your product as the bridge from the pain to the
  desired state.

## 2. Attention-Interest-Desire-Action (AIDA)

Best for: General landing pages where you need to move a user from curiosity to
commitment.

- **Attention:** A high-impact hero section with a clear value prop.
- **Interest:** Build engagement by showing how the solution works and why it's
  different.
- **Desire:** Use social proof, benefits, and "results" messaging to make the
  user want the outcome.
- **Action:** A clear, low-friction CTA.

## 3. The "High-Intent" Direct Flow

Best for: Users who are **Solution Aware** or **Most Aware** (e.g., coming from
a brand-specific search or a retargeting ad).

- **Value Statement:** Immediate confirmation that they are in the right place.
- **The "How it Works" (Quickly):** A 3-step visualization of the process.
- **Social Proof:** Immediate credibility (logos, ratings).
- **Direct CTA:** Asking for the conversion early, then following with
  supporting details for "scanners."

## 4. Persuasion Principles for Hierarchy

When structuring your sections, apply these core principles:

- **Primacy & Recency Effect:** Users remember the first and last things they
  see best. Place your most important benefit first and your strongest trust
  signal last (near the CTA).
- **Social Default:** If people are unsure, they look to others. Use "Trusted by
  X" early in the flow to set the "default" that your tool is the standard
  choice.
- **Choice Architecture:** Limit the number of paths. Every section should point
  toward the same primary goal.
- **Visual Salience:** Use color and size to ensure the eyes land on the most
  important persuasive claim in each section.

## Recommended Section Sequence (SaaS/Service)

1.  **Hero** (Attention/Value)
2.  **Social Proof Bar** (Baseline Trust)
3.  **The Problem/Context** (Relevance)
4.  **The Big Solution** (The "Aha" Moment)
5.  **Core Benefits** (Why it matters - 3 blocks)
6.  **Social Proof / Case Study** (Evidence of results)
7.  **How it Works** (Reducing functional anxiety)
8.  **Objection Handling / FAQ** (Removing friction)
9.  **Final CTA** (Action)
