# Relevance & Message Match Heuristics

These principles guide the creation of high-relevance user journeys from traffic
source to conversion.

## 1. The "Scent of Information" (Information Foraging)

Users act like predators following a scent. Every click must strengthen the
"scent" that they are getting closer to their goal. If the landing page doesn't
look or sound like the ad, the scent is lost, and the user "retreats" (bounces).

- **Implementation:** Repeat the exact "trigger words" from the source on the
  landing page.

## 2. The 3-Second Rule of Relevancy

A user decides whether a page is relevant to them within 3 seconds of landing.
If the H1 and Hero image do not confirm their intent immediately, you lose them.

- **Implementation:** Place the "Match" elements (H1, Primary Image, CTA) above
  the fold and in the center of the visual field.

## 3. Cognitive Load & The "Click Gap"

Every difference between the ad and the landing page (different colors,
different words, different offers) increases cognitive load. High cognitive load
leads to "Analysis Paralysis" or a "Bounce."

- **Implementation:** Standardize design tokens (colors, fonts, button styles)
  between ad creatives and landing page templates.

## 4. Expectation Management

The traffic source sets an expectation (a price, a benefit, a time-saving). The
landing page's primary job is to _fulfill_ that expectation before trying to
sell anything else.

- **Implementation:** If an ad promises "Free Shipping," the first thing the
  user sees should be a confirmation of that free shipping, not a list of other
  product features.

## 5. The "Mirror Test"

If you were to hold a mirror up to the ad and the landing page, how many
elements would reflect perfectly? The more "mirrored" elements, the higher the
conversion potential.

- **Mirror Points:**
  - Headline Keyword = H1 Keyword
  - CTA Action = Button Text
  - Ad Visual = Hero Visual
  - Ad Offer = Landing Page Price/Offer
