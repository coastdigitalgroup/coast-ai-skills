# Example: SaaS Ad-to-Landing-Page Journey

This example demonstrates the difference between a "Broken Scent" journey and a
"Strong Scent" journey for a hypothetical SaaS product called **"LeadFlow"** (a
lead management tool).

---

## Scenario

**Goal:** Drive sign-ups for a 14-day free trial. **Target Audience:** Real
estate agents struggling with lead follow-up. **The Ad Hook:** "Stop losing
leads to slow follow-up. Respond in seconds, not hours."

---

## 1. The "Broken Scent" (Low Performance)

### The Ad (Source)

- **Headline:** "Never lose another lead."
- **Visual:** A frustrated real estate agent looking at a phone.
- **CTA:** "Get LeadFlow."

### The Landing Page (Destination)

- **H1:** "The Enterprise-Grade Sales Acceleration Platform."
- **Subheadline:** "Scale your revenue with our integrated suite of CRM tools
  and AI-driven analytics."
- **Visual:** A generic stock photo of a modern office building.
- **CTA Button:** "Contact Us for a Quote."

### Why it Fails (The Friction)

- **Term Mismatch:** The ad uses simple language ("lose another lead"), while
  the page uses jargon ("Sales Acceleration," "Integrated Suite").
- **Visual Disconnect:** The agent is gone; the office building is irrelevant to
  the user's specific pain.
- **Offer Mismatch:** The user expected a tool ("Get LeadFlow"), but is met with
  a high-friction "Contact Us" request.
- **3-Second Result:** The user thinks, "This is too big/expensive for me," and
  bounces.

---

## 2. The "Strong Scent" (High Performance)

### The Ad (Source)

- **Headline:** "Stop losing leads to slow follow-up."
- **Visual:** A split screen: A "Missed Lead" notification on one side, and a
  "Lead Claimed" green check on the other.
- **CTA:** "Start My 14-Day Free Trial."

### The Landing Page (Destination)

- **H1:** "Respond to Real Estate Leads in Seconds, Not Hours."
- **Subheadline:** "Stop losing sales because you were too slow to call back.
  LeadFlow notifies you instantly so you can close the deal before they call a
  competitor."
- **Visual:** A close-up of the LeadFlow app interface showing a "New Lead"
  notification with a "Call Now" button.
- **CTA Button:** "Start My 14-Day Free Trial."

### Why it Succeeds (The Scent)

- **Keyword Continuity:** "Stop losing leads" and "seconds, not hours" are
  echoed immediately in the H1.
- **Visual Continuity:** The landing page shows the _solution_ to the ad's
  problem (the app in action).
- **Offer Continuity:** The CTA is identical to the ad's promise.
- **3-Second Result:** The user thinks, "This is exactly what I saw in the ad.
  This solves my problem," and scrolls down to learn more.

---

## Message Match Comparison Table

| Element         | Broken Scent               | Strong Scent               |
| :-------------- | :------------------------- | :------------------------- |
| **Headline**    | Vague & Jargon-heavy       | Echoes the ad hook exactly |
| **Imagery**     | Irrelevant stock photo     | Contextual product shot    |
| **Tone**        | Corporate/Stiff            | Direct/Problem-solving     |
| **CTA**         | High friction (Contact Us) | Low friction (Free Trial)  |
| **Scent Score** | 1/5                        | 5/5                        |
