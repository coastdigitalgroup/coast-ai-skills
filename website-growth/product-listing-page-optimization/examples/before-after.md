# Product Listing Page (PLP) Optimization: Before & After

## Scenario: Apparel Retailer Category Page

An online clothing store noticed that while their "Men's Jackets" category page
received high traffic, the progression rate to Product Detail Pages (PDPs) was
under 10%. Users were spending a lot of time scrolling but not clicking.

---

### Before: The "Information Desert"

**Characteristics:**

- **Filter Fatigue:** 12 filter categories (Size, Color, Material, Brand, Price,
  Season, Fit, Closure, Sleeve Length, Pattern, Occasion, Sustainable) all
  expanded by default on the left sidebar.
- **Sparse Product Cards:** Cards only showed the image, product name, and a
  small "View" button. Price was only visible on hover (desktop) or not at all
  (mobile).
- **Mobile Friction:** Filters were hidden behind a tiny text link that was hard
  to tap. No visual feedback when a filter was applied.
- **Inconsistent Imagery:** Some products had white backgrounds, others had
  lifestyle backgrounds, creating a jagged visual scanning path.

**Results:**

- **CTR to PDP:** 8.5%
- **Filter Usage:** 4% (Users felt overwhelmed by the sidebar)
- **Pogo-sticking:** 45% (Users clicked products just to see the price, then
  immediately went back)

---

### After: The "Guided Discovery" Hub

**Improvements Applied:**

- **Applied the "3-Filter Rule":** Only "Size," "Color," and "Price" were
  expanded by default. The rest were collapsed under a "More Filters" section.
- **Enhanced Product Cards:**
  - Added visible **Price** and **Star Rating** to every card.
  - Added **Color Swatches** so users could see variants without clicking.
  - Standardized all images to a consistent 4:5 aspect ratio with light gray
    backgrounds.
- **Mobile First Navigation:**
  - Implemented a sticky "Filter & Sort" bar at the bottom of the screen.
  - Used a full-screen drawer for filtering with large touch targets.
  - Added "Active Filter Tokens" at the top of the grid so users could see what
    was applied.
- **Smart Sorting:** Changed default sort from "Alphabetical" to "Best Sellers"
  weighted by inventory levels.

**Results:**

- **CTR to PDP:** 21% (+147% lift)
- **Filter Usage:** 18% (Streamlined sidebar encouraged interaction)
- **Pogo-sticking:** 12% (Users had enough info to "pre-qualify" their clicks)

---

### Key Takeaway

By reducing the interaction cost of filtering and increasing the information
density of the product cards, the site allowed users to find relevant products
faster, leading to higher intent clicks and better funnel progression.
