# Example: Reducing Discovery Friction on a High-Volume Apparel PLP

## Scenario: The "Summer Dress" Collection

An apparel brand noticed that while their "Summer Dresses" category had high
traffic, the bounce rate was 65% and the Product Detail Page (PDP) click-through
rate was only 8%.

### Before Optimization (The "Friction" State)

- **Filters:** All filters (Size, Color, Price, Length) were hidden behind a
  single "Filter" button, even on desktop.
- **Sorting:** Defaulted to "Alphabetical," showing accessories (e.g., "A-line
  Belts") before popular dresses.
- **Product Cards:** Only showed the product name and price. No indication of
  available sizes or colors.
- **Mobile:** Used standard pagination (1, 2, 3...) at the bottom of the page,
  requiring users to click and wait for a reload.

### The Audit Findings

1. Users were clicking the "Filter" button but abandoning when presented with
   15+ options at once.
2. 40% of PDP bounces were because the user's size was out of stock—information
   they didn't have on the PLP.
3. Mobile users rarely went past Page 1.

---

### After Optimization (The "Discovery" State)

- **Exposed Filters:** On desktop, "Size" and "Color" filters were moved to a
  left-hand sidebar and expanded by default.
- **Smart Sorting:** The default was changed to "Best Sellers."
- **Rich Product Cards:**
  - Added **Variant Swatches:** Users can see available colors directly on the
    card.
  - Added **"Quick Add" Size Selection:** A small overlay appears on hover (or
    tap) allowing users to add to cart without leaving the PLP.
  - Added **Low Stock Indicators:** "Only 2 left" displayed for popular sizes.
- **Mobile Navigation:** Replaced pagination with a "Load More" button and
  implemented "Sticky Filters" that stayed at the top of the viewport.

### Results

- **PDP Click-Through Rate:** Increased from 8% to 14% (75% lift).
- **Add-to-Cart (ATC) from PLP:** "Quick Add" accounted for 20% of all ATCs.
- **Bounce Rate:** Dropped from 65% to 48%.
- **Filter Interaction:** 3x increase in users selecting a size before browsing.
