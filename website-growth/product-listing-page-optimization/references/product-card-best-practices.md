# Product Card Best Practices

The product card is the most important component on the PLP. It must provide
enough information to trigger a click without creating visual clutter.

## 1. Information Architecture

A high-converting product card follows this hierarchy:

1. **The Hero Image:** Should be the largest element. For apparel, use lifestyle
   or model shots. For tech, use clean studio shots.
2. **Brand/Category (Optional):** Small, subtle text at the top.
3. **Product Name:** 1-2 lines maximum.
4. **Price:** Bold and high contrast. If on sale, show the original price struck
   through and the discount percentage.
5. **Social Proof:** Star ratings (e.g., 4.5/5) and the number of reviews in
   parentheses.

## 2. Interaction States

- **Hover (Desktop):** Reveal secondary image, "Quick Add" buttons, or variant
  options.
- **Tap (Mobile):** The primary tap area should be the image and title.
  Secondary actions like "Add to Cart" should be distinct buttons.

## 3. Variant Representation

- If a product has < 5 colors, show them as small circular swatches.
- If a product has > 5 colors, show the first 4 and a "+X more" indicator.
- Clicking a swatch should update the hero image on the card instantly.

## 4. "Quick Action" Heuristics

- **Quick View:** Use for complex products (furniture, tech) where more info is
  needed before ATC.
- **Quick Add:** Use for simple products (consumables, basic apparel) where the
  user already knows their requirements.
