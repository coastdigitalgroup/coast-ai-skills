# Product Discovery Heuristics

Optimizing a Product Listing Page (PLP) is about balancing **Information
Abundance** with **Cognitive Load**.

## 1. The Pogo-Sticking Heuristic

"Pogo-sticking" occurs when a user clicks a product, realizes it’s not what they
wanted, and immediately clicks back.

- **The Fix:** Increase information density on the PLP card.
- **Rule of Thumb:** If users are clicking back within 5 seconds, they didn't
  have enough information on the listing page to make an informed click. Add
  price, ratings, or key specs (e.g., "100% Cotton") to the card.

## 2. Faceted Search UX (The Paradox of Choice)

Too many options lead to decision paralysis.

- **Progressive Disclosure:** Hide less-frequently used filters behind a "See
  More" link within a category, or collapse the entire category.
- **The 3-Filter Rule:** Project memory indicates that expanding the top three
  filters (usually Size, Price, and one high-intent category like Color or
  Brand) provides the best balance of guidance and screen real estate.

## 3. Mobile Grid Strategy

The choice between a 1-column and 2-column grid depends on the **Visual vs.
Functional** nature of the product.

- **1-Column (Single Stack):** Best for products where visual detail is the
  primary driver (e.g., intricate jewelry, detailed artwork).
- **2-Column (Side-by-Side):** Best for "Scannable" products where users want to
  compare many items quickly (e.g., T-shirts, electronics, groceries).

## 4. Sorting Intent

Users sort for different reasons.

- **"Best Match" (Default):** Should be a mix of popularity, relevance to the
  search term, and current inventory.
- **"Newest":** Vital for return visitors who want to see what's changed since
  their last visit.
- **"Price Low-High":** Often used by bargain hunters; ensure "Sale" items are
  correctly indexed here.

## 5. Trust Signals at Scale

Individual reviews matter on the PDP, but **Aggregated Proof** matters on the
PLP.

- **Star Ratings:** Essential for building trust in the grid.
- **"Best Seller" Badges:** Acts as a heuristic for "this is a safe choice,"
  reducing the mental effort of selection.
