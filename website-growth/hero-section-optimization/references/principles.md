# Hero Optimization References & Principles

This document contains key heuristics and principles for designing and auditing high-performing hero sections.

## 1. The 5-Second Test (UX Heuristic)
A visitor should be able to answer three questions within 5 seconds of landing on your page:
1. **What is this?** (Product/Service category)
2. **Who is it for?** (Target audience)
3. **What do I do next?** (The Call to Action)

If they can't answer these, your hero section is failing on clarity.

---

## 2. The Value Proposition Formula
A strong value proposition (H1 + Subheadline) follows this structure:
- **Headline (H1):** [Result] for [Target Audience] via [Unique Mechanism].
- **Subheadline:** [Product Name] is a [Category] that helps you [Primary Benefit] without [Major Pain Point].

---

## 3. Visual Hierarchy & The "F-Pattern"
Most users scan web pages in an "F" or "Z" pattern.
- **Top Left:** Logo (Identity).
- **Top Right:** Navigation/Secondary CTA.
- **Center/Left Center:** Headline & Value Prop.
- **Directly Below Value Prop:** Primary CTA.

**Rule:** Ensure the Primary CTA has the highest visual weight (contrast, size, and surrounding negative space).

---

## 4. Trust Patterns
To increase conversion in the hero section, use "Micro-Trust" signals:
- **Quantifiable Proof:** "Join 5,000+ happy customers."
- **Authority:** "As featured in [TechCrunch/Forbes]."
- **Risk Reversal:** "No credit card required," "Cancel anytime," or "Free shipping."
- **Social Proof:** A single, powerful testimonial quote or star rating.

---

## 5. CTA Copywriting Principles
Avoid "Low-Intent" or "Generic" buttons.
- **Bad:** Submit, Get Started, Click Here, Learn More.
- **Good:** Start My Free Trial, Get My Custom Quote, Claim My Discount, Show Me the Results.

**Tip:** Complete the sentence "I want to..." to find your CTA text. (e.g., "I want to... Start My Free Trial").
