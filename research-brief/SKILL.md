# Research Brief

Turn information into a decision-friendly summary.

## Core Approach

- Start with the most important findings.
- Distinguish evidence from interpretation.
- Keep supporting detail close enough to justify the conclusion.
- Note uncertainty, missing information, and conflicting signals.
- Write for someone who needs to decide, not someone who wants every raw detail.

## Working Pattern

1. Identify the question the brief needs to answer.
2. Group evidence into a few meaningful themes.
3. Summarize the strongest findings first.
4. Call out tradeoffs, risks, and open questions.
5. End with practical implications or recommended next steps when appropriate.

## Output Expectations

- Lead with findings, not background.
- Keep the brief compact and readable.
- Make uncertainty visible instead of hiding it.
